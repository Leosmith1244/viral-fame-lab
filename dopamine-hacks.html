Dopamine Hacks

Trigger shares by evoking outrage, awe, or FOMO

Speaker: Club President

Objective: Teach how to trigger shares by evoking outrage, awe, or FOMO

Speech Outline

  • Introduction (1 minute)
    • The science behind viral content
    • Introduction to emotional triggers
  • Body (4-5 minutes)
    • Why Emotions Drive Shares: Psychology and data
    • Three Dopamine Hacks: Outrage, Awe, FOMO
    • Real-World Success: Club example
  • Conclusion (1 minute)
    • Recap of key points
    • Call to action: Craft one post with these hacks

Introduction

Imagine posting a video that explodes with shares, flooding your notifications overnight. It's not luck—it's science. As Club President, I've seen our posts go viral by tapping into human emotions. Last year, one post tripled our event turnout in a week. Today, I'll teach you how to trigger shares by evoking outrage, awe, or FOMO, using Aristotle's Rhetorical Triangle—ethos, pathos, and logos. Get ready to make your content unstoppable.

Why Emotions Drive Shares

Emotions are the engine of virality. Harvard Business Review found that posts evoking strong emotions—like anger or awe—are 20% more likely to go viral. When you feel something, your brain releases dopamine, a chemical linked to pleasure and motivation. That dopamine hit makes you share, spreading the emotion. For example, a post about a shocking injustice (outrage) or a breathtaking achievement (awe) gets shared because it makes people feel and connect.

Three Dopamine Hacks

Here are three dopamine hacks to make your content shareable:

1. Outrage (Pathos)

Tap into anger or frustration about a common problem. A post exposing a scam or unfairness sparks outrage, driving shares to warn others. Use pathos to stir emotion, but keep it ethical—focus on issues, not attacks.

Example: A consumer rights video: "This company charges $50 for a $5 product—here's how to fight back!" It went viral with 10K shares, using pathos to fuel outrage.

2. Awe (Ethos + Pathos)

Showcase something amazing to inspire wonder. A post about a record-breaking feat or a stunning discovery evokes awe, making people share to spread inspiration. Combine ethos (credibility) with pathos (emotion) for impact.

Example: A travel video: "I climbed Everest in 24 hours—here's the unseen footage." It garnered 50K shares, using awe to build ethos and pathos.

3. FOMO (Logos + Pathos)

Create Fear Of Missing Out on a benefit or event. A post highlighting limited-time offers or exclusive insights triggers FOMO, pushing shares to secure access. Use logos (logic) to show the value and pathos to amplify urgency.

Example: A club event post: "Only 5 spots left for our exclusive workshop—don't miss out!" It boosted sign-ups by 40%, using FOMO with logos and pathos.

Real-World Success

Our club used these hacks to promote a public speaking workshop. We posted a video with:

  • Outrage: "Most people fear public speaking—here's why the system fails you" (pathos).
  • Awe: Showcased a member's transformation from shy to confident speaker (ethos + pathos).
  • FOMO: "Limited seats—register now!" (logos + pathos).

Result: The post got shared 200+ times, filling the event and attracting 15 new members. It worked because it hit all three emotional triggers, aligned with Aristotle's principles.

Conclusion

Emotions drive shares—outrage sparks action, awe inspires wonder, and FOMO creates urgency. Use pathos, ethos, and logos to craft posts that trigger dopamine and go viral. Tonight, pick one hack and create a post for our club. Let's make our content contagious!

Activity: Rewrite a Dull Post

Transform a boring club post into a shareable hit

Instructions

Let's practice turning a dull Toastmasters post into a viral contender using outrage, awe, or FOMO:

Original Post (Dull Version)

"Join our Toastmasters meeting next Tuesday at 7 PM. We'll practice public speaking. All are welcome."

Why It Flops: No emotion, no urgency, no hook—just facts. It's easily skipped.

Activity Steps

  1. Divide into 3-4 groups (in-person or virtual breakout rooms)
  2. Each group rewrites the post using one dopamine hack:
    • Group 1: Use outrage (e.g., "Tired of boring meetings that waste your time?")
    • Group 2: Use awe (e.g., "Watch ordinary people become confident speakers in 60 minutes!")
    • Group 3: Use FOMO (e.g., "Only 3 spots left for our life-changing session—claim yours now!")
  3. You have 3 minutes to craft your version
  4. Regroup and share one rewritten post per group (30 seconds each)

Example Rewrites

Outrage Version

"Most public speaking advice is garbage—it leaves you nervous and unprepared. We're fixing that Tuesday at 7 PM. Join us and speak with confidence!"

Why It Works: Pathos-driven outrage hooks viewers and promises a solution.

Awe Version

"From stage fright to standing ovation—see the transformation at our Toastmasters meeting Tuesday! Witness real people becoming powerful speakers."

Why It Works: Ethos and pathos combine to inspire awe and credibility.

FOMO Version

"Our Tuesday workshop is almost full! Don't miss your chance to unlock public speaking secrets—only 5 spots remain. Secure yours before it's gone!"

Why It Works: Logos and pathos create urgency and value.

Summary: Amazing job! We transformed a bland post into shareable content by tapping into emotions. Use these hacks to make every post count.
