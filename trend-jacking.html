Trend-Jacking Olympics

Leverage trending memes or topics to promote Toastmasters

Speaker: VP of Education

Objective: Teach how to leverage trending memes or topics to promote Toastmasters and increase engagement

Speech Outline

  • Introduction (1 minute)
    • The power of trends in social media
    • Introduction to trend-jacking
  • Body (4-5 minutes)
    • Why Trend-Jacking Works: Psychology and data
    • Three Trend-Jacking Strategies: Memes, challenges, news
    • Real-World Success: Club example
  • Conclusion (1 minute)
    • Recap of key points
    • Call to action: Create one trend-jacked post

Introduction

Think of the last viral trend you saw—maybe a dance challenge or a meme format. Now imagine using that buzz to promote Toastmasters, riding the wave to reach thousands. As VP of Education, I've seen trend-jacking boost our club's visibility overnight. Last quarter, a single trend-jacked post brought in 20 new inquiries. Today, I'll teach you how to leverage trending memes or topics using Aristotle's Rhetorical Triangle. Let's turn trends into traffic for our club.

Why Trend-Jacking Works

Trend-jacking works because it taps into existing cultural momentum. When you align with a trend, you join a conversation that's already engaging millions. Psychology shows that people are drawn to familiar patterns—trends feel relevant and timely. For example, during the "Barbie" movie craze, brands that incorporated pink themes saw a 30% spike in engagement. By trend-jacking, you gain visibility without starting from scratch, making your content feel current and relatable.

Three Trend-Jacking Strategies

Here are three strategies to effectively jack trends for Toastmasters promotion:

1. Meme Formats (Pathos)

Adapt popular meme templates to your message. Memes use humor and relatability (pathos) to connect quickly. For instance, the "Distracted Boyfriend" meme could show someone ignoring boring speeches for Toastmasters' engaging sessions.

Example: A post using the "They Don't Know" meme: "Meanwhile, at Toastmasters: people actually enjoying public speaking." It got 1K shares, using pathos to spark laughter and interest.

2. Challenge Participation (Logos + Pathos)

Join viral challenges with a Toastmasters twist. Challenges often have clear rules (logos) and emotional appeal (pathos). For example, the "Put a Finger Down" challenge could become "Put a Finger Down if You've Ever Frozen During a Speech," then promote our solutions.

Example: A video: "We did the 'Glow Up' challenge—watch our members transform from nervous to confident speakers!" It garnered 5K views, using logos (structured challenge) and pathos (emotional journey).

3. Newsjacking (Ethos)

Link to breaking news or events to show relevance. Newsjacking builds credibility (ethos) by positioning your club as timely and informed. For instance, during a major conference, post about how Toastmasters prepares you for such events.

Example: A post during a political debate: "Wish you could speak as confidently as these candidates? Join Toastmasters to learn how." It received 200 shares, using ethos to tie into current events.

Real-World Success

Our club used these strategies during the "Wordle" craze. We created a post: "Today's Wordle: SPEAK. Struggling to find the right words? Toastmasters helps you articulate better every day." It combined:

  • Meme Format: Wordle grid (pathos).
  • Challenge Participation: Implied "solve this" (logos).
  • Newsjacking: Tapping into a daily trend (ethos).

Result: The post went viral in local groups, doubling our meeting attendance that week. It worked because it leveraged a familiar trend with a clear Toastmasters link, aligning with Aristotle's principles.

Conclusion

Trend-jacking uses memes for emotion, challenges for structure, and news for credibility. It amplifies your reach by riding cultural waves. Tonight, pick one trending topic and create a post for our club. Let's make our content timely, relatable, and unstoppable!

Activity: Meme Rebrand Race

Rebrand a trend for a club event in breakout rooms

Instructions

Let's practice trend-jacking by rebranding a popular trend for a Toastmasters event—our upcoming "Impromptu Speaking Workshop":

Current Trend: "Silent Library" Challenge

Trend Description: People try not to laugh or make noise while watching something funny—often failing hilariously.

Workshop Details: Learn to think on your feet with fun, quick-speaking exercises in a supportive environment.

Activity Steps

  1. Divide into 3-4 groups (in-person or virtual breakout rooms)
  2. Each group rebrands the "Silent Library" trend to promote the workshop:
    • Option A: Create a meme format (e.g., "When you're in Silent Library vs. When you're in our Impromptu Workshop")
    • Option B: Design a challenge (e.g., "Can you stay serious during our impromptu laughs? Try it!")
    • Option C: Newsjack it (e.g., "Everyone's doing Silent Library—but we're turning it into a speaking skill!")
  3. You have 3 minutes to brainstorm and sketch your idea
  4. Regroup and share one idea per group (30 seconds each)
  5. We'll vote for the most creative trend-jack!

Example Ideas

Meme Format Idea

"Me trying not to laugh in Silent Library" (image of strained face) vs. "Me having fun while speaking impromptu at Toastmasters" (image of confident smile).

Why It Works: Pathos-driven humor contrasts fear with confidence, promoting the workshop's benefits.

Challenge Idea

"The Impromptu Laugh Challenge: Try our quick-speaking exercises without cracking up! Tag us in your videos."

Why It Works: Logos (structured challenge) and pathos (fun) encourage participation and shares.

Newsjacking Idea

"Silent Library is trending, but silence won't help you in meetings. Join our workshop to speak up confidently!"

Why It Works: Ethos ties to a current trend while highlighting the workshop's value.

Summary: Brilliant work! We've seen how to creatively jack trends for club promotion. Use these strategies to stay relevant and engaging in the fast-paced social media world.
